Frequently Asked Questions

Why does REPS Shield need background location access?

REPS Shield automatically detects when you drive to your rental properties — like Strava tracks runs. Without background location, the app can't detect trips when you're driving and haven't opened the app first. The IRS requires contemporaneous records, which means logging trips as they happen, not reconstructing them later. Background location is the only way to do this reliably.

GPS isn't detecting my trips. What do I do?

First, check your location permissions: go to your phone's Settings → Privacy → Location Services → REPS Shield and make sure it's set to "Always" (not "While Using"). Also make sure Motion & Fitness is enabled. On Android, check that battery optimization is disabled for the app.

Are my personal trip routes stored?

No. If you classify a trip as personal, the route data is immediately and permanently deleted. We never store routes for personal trips. Only work-classified trips have their routes retained, and only for the purpose of IRS mileage documentation.

How do I share my records with my CPA?

Go to Reports → Generate CPA Export Pack → Share with CPA. This creates a secure, read-only PDF link that expires in 30 days. Your CPA doesn't need an account — they just click the link.

What is the 750-hour test?

Under IRC §469(c)(7), to qualify for Real Estate Professional Status you must spend more than 750 hours per year in real property trades or businesses. REPS Shield tracks this in real time and shows your progress on the home screen. This is not tax advice — consult your CPA for your specific situation.

Can I use REPS Shield on multiple devices?

Yes. Your account syncs across devices in real time. Log on your phone, review on your desktop dashboard, generate exports anywhere.

How do I delete my account and data?

Go to Settings → Data & Privacy → Delete Account. Type "DELETE" to confirm. All your data is permanently deleted within 30 days. This action cannot be undone.

How long should I keep my REPS records?

The IRS can audit returns up to 3 years back, or 6 years if there's a substantial understatement of income. We recommend retaining REPS records for at least 7 years. REPS Shield recommends this retention period but the decision is yours — you can delete records at any time.

Is REPS Shield a tax filing tool?

No. REPS Shield is a documentation and record-keeping tool only. It does not file taxes, provide tax advice, or guarantee IRS qualification. Always work with a qualified CPA or tax attorney for your specific situation.
